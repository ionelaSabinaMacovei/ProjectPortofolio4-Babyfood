Recipes for Babies and Toddlers

Browse Categories

From 6 months-old, if you started your baby solid food introduction at the age of 4 months, your baby now knows a good list of foods and you have concocted amazing recipes! The texture of the puree can now be slightly granular but still quite smooth and easy to swallow for your baby.
At the age of 7 months, you realize that your baby is growing sooooo fast… and that it is necessary to adapt his diet, and quantities to meet his new needs. 7-months baby recipes are fun to cook! Your baby will continue drinking milk. For the quantities of puree, listen to your baby: try to give him a little more as you go but never force your baby to finish his puree. At this age, baby should eat about 6oz of puree per day, but again it depends on the babies, their size, their weight … always adapt these quantities to your baby.
From the age of 8 months, your baby will discover new textures. Indeed, you will be able to give him food cut into very small pieces in his baby recipes. Vegetables will now be crushed, about 6.3oz per day. The amount of meat or fish will be able to increase also from 0.35oz to 5oz per meal (preferably only for lunch).
From 9 months old, your baby is taller and starts eating more fruits and vegetables. The fruits can be given raw, finely sliced for your baby to discover new textures. We give 7oz of vegetables for lunchtime with meat, fish or egg. The egg can also be introduced with the white, hard (1 third of hard egg during lunch, not more than once a week).
At the age of 10 months, your baby increases his daily rations, and you continue giving him more small pieces in his baby recipes. Vegetables are crushed and the meat or fish crushed into small pieces in its recipes.
At the age of 11 months, your baby is growing fast and his needs increase. You give him more small pieces in his baby recipes. Vegetables are well crushed after cooking and the fish or meat crushed into small pieces. You can also give an egg for lunch instead of meat and fish.
At the age of 12 months, your baby increases his daily rations, and you continue to give him more small pieces in his baby recipes. Your baby can even starts eating alone and we love watching him fend for himself like a big one! Your baby can start eating more fruits and vegetables. Exotic fruits for example, such as persimmon, or passion fruit.

About Us

This blog is for anyone who loves to cook and for everyone who doesn’t.

Our goal here is to publish recipes that you, your family and your friends will not only love but will want to make time and time again. We hope to inspire you as a home cook and boost your confidence in the kitchen. Life is meant to be enjoyed and doesn’t good food just make it more enjoyable? Please follow along and let's create something delicious!

We think we have built a good food blog, so naturally, you’re going to find recipes here. But we only share the recipes we really, truly love. You won’t find anything here that we’ve never made before or would never make again. Every recipe we post has our stamp of approval. If we wouldn’t eat it or serve it, you won’t find it here. Step-by-step instructions with photos. When we first started cooking, we were unsure about so many things: How big should we dice the onions? How thick should we cut the vegetables? What should this look like halfway through the recipe when it looks nothing like the final photo? Helpful tips and techniques. We love learning any skill that helps us get the job done faster, easier or with a better result. That’s why we created a page of nothing but useful tips and techniques. Fun! Food and fun come together in this blog because everything about food should be fun: eating it, making it and even making a mess of it.

Enjoy!!
